verifyText css=#contents > p The name Bluetooth was named after 10th century Viking king in Denmark Harald Bluetooth who united and controlled Denmark and Norway [i]. Bluetooth is a low power, low cost, and short range radio network standard. It supports unlicensed Industrial, Scientific and Medical (ISM) 2.4 GHz short-range radio frequency band. It is complementary to the Wi-Fi network specified by IEEE 802.11b/g/a standard. Bluetooth uses a frequency hopping scheme to provide robust wireless communication. As a cable replacement, Bluetooth is widely used in cell phone, PDA, laptop, headset, and printer, etc. to form a Personal Area Network (PAN) and provide universal access . Bluetooth uses a radio technology called frequency-hopping spread spectrum [ii].
